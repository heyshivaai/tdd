CATALOG MATCHING RULES:
- If your observation clearly maps to a catalog signal (e.g., you found SOC2 compliance issues → CC-03), set catalog_signal_id to that ID and catalog_match to true
- If it's a close but imperfect match, still set catalog_signal_id but set catalog_match to false and use confidence MEDIUM
- If no catalog signal fits, set catalog_signal_id to null and catalog_match to false
- Use the catalog signal's conviction_weight and temporal_orientation when matched

RATING GUIDE (calibrated from practitioner comparison — avoid over-rating):
- RED: Deal-breaker or significant remediation cost (>$500K or 6+ months to fix). Reserve RED for findings that would change deal terms or kill the deal. Examples: active data breaches, critical compliance failures, platform unsuitability.
- YELLOW: Meaningful concern, manageable with plan. Most operational risks belong here — cost volatility, process gaps, DR readiness concerns, QA bottlenecks, vendor management gaps. When in doubt between RED and YELLOW, prefer YELLOW unless there is direct evidence of active harm or regulatory violation.
- GREEN: Positive signal or well-managed area. Certifications in place, strong retention, mature processes.

SEVERITY CALIBRATION (learned from Horizon engagement):
- Do NOT rate cost volatility as RED unless there is evidence of budget overrun or financial distress — cost variance alone is YELLOW
- Do NOT rate DR/failover gaps as RED unless the company has had actual downtime incidents — missing formal RTO/RPO documentation alone is YELLOW
- Do NOT rate QA process gaps as RED unless there are documented quality incidents — understaffed QA alone is YELLOW
- DO rate active data incidents (PHI breaches, PII leaks) as RED — even one confirmed breach in healthcare is material
- DO rate missing AI governance as YELLOW when AI tools are in production use (CRITICAL only if AI directly affects patient/customer outcomes)
- When a compliance certification is current (SOC2, HITRUST, HIPAA), do NOT override to RED based on individual control gaps alone — those are YELLOW findings within a GREEN domain

CONFIDENCE GUIDE:
- HIGH: Clear evidence in quoted text
- MEDIUM: Inferred from context — evidence is suggestive but not explicit
- LOW: Weak signal — noted but uncertain
