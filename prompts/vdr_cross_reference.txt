COMPOUND RISK PATTERNS TO LOOK FOR (v1.4 calibration-enhanced):
When synthesizing signals into compound risks, actively look for these cross-pillar patterns that practitioners commonly identify:

1. CUSTOMER CONCENTRATION + LEGACY PLATFORM: A top customer (>10% CARR) on a legacy/deprecated platform creates compound risk — losing the customer during migration is a revenue event.
2. DATA INCIDENTS + COMPLIANCE CERTIFICATION: Active PHI/PII breaches alongside current compliance certifications (SOC2, HITRUST) = governance gap. The certification doesn't protect against active incidents.
3. MULTI-CLOUD + COST GROWTH: Running 3+ environments (AWS + GCP + on-prem) with linear cost scaling = compounding infra spend that won't self-correct without consolidation investment.
4. ORG FRAGMENTATION + TECH DEBT: Fragmented tech leadership (no single CTO) + high tech debt = nobody owns the remediation. Debt grows because no one has authority to prioritize it.
5. SECURITY TOOL GAPS + INCIDENT DETECTION DELAYS: Missing SIEM/SOC + slow MTTD = compound security exposure. Each gap amplifies the other.
6. QA BOTTLENECK + DEPLOYMENT VELOCITY: Small/manual QA team + high deployment frequency = release quality risk. Look for test automation gaps alongside rapid release cycles.

SEVERITY CALIBRATION FOR COMPOUND RISKS:
- CRITICAL: Reserve for compound risks where the combination creates deal-value impact (>$1M remediation or >12 months to resolve) — NOT just because individual signals are RED
- HIGH: Compound risk that requires attention in first 100 days post-acquisition
- MEDIUM: Compound risk that should be on the value creation roadmap but isn't urgent
